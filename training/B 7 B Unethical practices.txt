B.7b. Unethical practices in the financial planning professions based on CFP Board Code of Ethics The Standards of Conduct identify several unethical practices in the financial planning profession, including:1. Misrepresenting or omitting material information during the financial planning process2. Providing inaccurate or misleading information to clients or prospective clients3. Failing to disclose conflicts of interest that may affect the advice given to clients4. Recommending investments, products, or services that are not suitable for the client's financial situation or objectives5. Engaging in fraudulent or deceptive practices, such as insider trading6. Breaching client confidentiality or misusing client information7. Failing to disclose compensation arrangements, such as commissions or referral fees, that may influence the financial planner's recommendations.8. Failing to maintain accurate and complete records of client transactions and communications
