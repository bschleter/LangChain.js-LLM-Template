A.4A. Regulatory authorities that impact elements of the financial planning process1. Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA): FINRA is a self-regulatory organization that oversees securities firms and brokers in the United States. It sets standards for ethical and professional conduct, and enforces compliance with these standards through disciplinary actions. CFP must comply with securities laws. 2. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC): The SEC is a federal agency that regulates the securities industry in the United States. It oversees publicly traded companies and investment advisers, and enforces securities laws to protect investors. A CFP must comply with securities and exchange laws and regulations. 3. State Insurance Departments: Each state has its own insurance department that regulates insurance companies and agents within the state. These departments oversee insurance sales practices, investigate complaints, and enforce state insurance laws.4. State Boards of Accountancy: State boards of accountancy oversee the licensing and regulation of certified public accountants (CPAs) in each state. They set education and experience requirements for CPAs, and enforce ethical standards for the profession.5. State Bar Associations: State bar associations regulate the practice of law within each state. They oversee the licensing of attorneys, set ethical standards for the profession, and investigate complaints of attorney misconduct. Ethical standards relating to the bar association relates to similar ethical standards in which a CFP processional must cary themselves. 6. Department of Labor (DOL): The DOL oversees retirement plans, including 401(k) plans and pension plans. It enforces laws related to these plans, such as the Employee Retirement Income Security Act (ERISA). 7. Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB): The CFPB is a federal agency that regulates consumer financial products and services, including mortgages, credit cards, and student loans. It enforces consumer protection laws and investigates complaints of financial fraud.8. Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC): The FDIC is a federal agency that insures deposits at banks and savings associations. It also supervises these institutions to ensure their safety and soundness. Their insurance limits influence how much we may advise a client to leave in a protected bank account and how bank accounts are separated. 9. National Credit Union Administration (NCUA): The NCUA is a federal agency that regulates credit unions in the United States. It insures deposits at credit unions and supervises these institutions to ensure their safety and soundness.10. Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC): The OCC is a federal agency that regulates national banks and federal savings associations. It supervises these institutions to ensure their safety and soundness, and enforces compliance with federal banking laws.
