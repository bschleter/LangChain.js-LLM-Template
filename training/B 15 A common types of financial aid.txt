B.15A. Common types of financial aidThere are several types of financial aid available to students who need help paying for college. Some of the most common types of financial aid include:1. Scholarships - Scholarships are gifts of money that do not need to be repaid. They are typically awarded based on merit, financial need, or other criteria.2. Grants - Grants are similar to scholarships in that they do not need to be repaid. They are typically awarded based on financial need, an albeit harder if you the typical client of a financial planner. These are typically PELL Grants for students who are dependent or with lower income parents. 3. Work-Study - Work-study programs allow students to work on or off campus to earn money to pay for college expenses. These jobs are often related to the student's field of study, and great options for high school seniors. 4. Parent Loans - Parent loans, also known as Parent PLUS loans, are loans that are offered to parents of dependent undergraduate students. These loans can be used to pay for college expenses that are not covered by other forms of financial aid.
